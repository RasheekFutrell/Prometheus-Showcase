Rule of 72
Divide 72 by your annual return to find how long money takes to double. At 7%, your money doubles in about 10.3 years. At 10%, just 7.2 years. This is why rate of return matters more than most people realise.
Debt-to-Income Ratio
DTI is total monthly debt payments divided by gross monthly income. Lenders want below 36%. Below 20% puts you in excellent standing. Above 43%, most mortgage lenders will decline regardless of credit score.
The 4% Withdrawal Rule
You can withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually without running out over 30 years. Multiply your annual spending by 25 to get your FIRE number. Spend $48,000/year? You need $1.2M invested.
Tax-Advantaged Account Order
Capture the full 401k employer match first. Then HSA if eligible. Then IRA. Then back to 401k. Taxable brokerage last. Every step out of order costs you money.
